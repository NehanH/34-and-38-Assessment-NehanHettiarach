Texturing

What is Blender Texturing?

Texturing in Blender is the process of applying images, colors, or procedural effects to the surfaces of 3D objects to make them appear more realistic. This can include adding details like color, roughness, metallic shine, and more to the surface of an object. Texturing is a crucial part of 3D modelling and rendering, as it adds depth and detail to the models and makes them look more realistic.

Task 1

Basic Texturing: Start by applying basic colors to a cube or sphere.

Task 2

Image Texturing: Learn how to apply an image texture to a cube or sphere. Make sure when finding your image online that it is free to use. This will be very important when doing your assignment. (Legal Implications)

Task 3

UV Mapping: UV mapping is the process of projecting a 2D image onto a 3D model's surface. Try unwrapping a square and applying an image to one of its sides.

Task 4

Procedural Texturing: Procedural textures are generated using mathematical algorithms rather than images. Experiment with creating and applying procedural textures to understand how they can be used to create complex and detailed models

Task 5

Texture Painting: Blender has a feature which lets you paint directly onto a 3D model. Use this feature to add some details to a cube, like leopard print or your initials.
