Texturing

What is Blender Texturing?

Texturing in Blender is the process of applying images, colors, or procedural effects to the surfaces of 3D objects to make them appear more realistic. This can include adding details like color, roughness, metallic shine, and more to the surface of an object. Texturing is a crucial part of 3D modelling and rendering, as it adds depth and detail to the models and makes them look more realistic.

Task 1

Basic Texturing: Start by applying basic colors to a cube or sphere.

1. Open Blender and create a new project

2. Add a simple object to your scene, like a cube or sphere

3. In the properties panel, select the material tab

4. Click on the 'New' button to create a new material

5. In the 'Surface' section, change the 'Base Color' to your desired color.

Task 2

Image Texturing: Learn how to apply an image texture to a cube or sphere. Make sure when finding your image online that it is free to use. This will be very important when doing your assignment. (Legal Implications)

1. Find an image to use

2. In the properties panel, select the material tab and create a new material

3. In the 'Surface' section, next to 'Base Color', click on the small dot and select 'Image Texture'

4. Click 'Open' and select your desired image texture from your files.

Task 3

UV Mapping: UV mapping is the process of projecting a 2D image onto a 3D model's surface. Try unwrapping a square and applying an image to one of its sides.

1. Switch to Edit Mode and select all vertices (Shortcut: A)

2. Open the UV editor view in a new window

3. In the 3D view, press U to unwrap the model. Select a method that suits your model. (e.g., 'Unwrap'. 'Smart UV Project')

In the UV editor, open your texture image, you should see the UV map of your model overlaid on the image.

Task 4

Procedural Texturing: Procedural textures are generated using mathematical algorithms rather than images. Experiment with creating and applying procedural textures to understand how they can be used to create complex and detailed models

1. In the properties panel, select the material tab and create a new material

2. In the 'Surface' section, next to 'Base Color', click on the small dot and select 'Noise Texture' or another procedural texture

3. Adjust the settings of the procedural texture to achieve your desired effect

Task 5

Texture Painting: Blender has a feature which lets you paint directly onto a 3D model. Use this feature to add some details to a cube, like leopard print or your initials.

1. Switch to texture paint mode

2. In the properties panel, select the material tab and create a new material

3. In the 'Surface' section, next to 'Base Color', click on the small dot and select 'Image Texture'

Click 'New' to create a new image to paint on

Use the brushes in the tool panel to paint directly onto your model.
